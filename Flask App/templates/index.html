Overview

  • Trader Joe's and Walmart couldn't be more different as stores in terms of the demographics they target, the products they sell, the size of stores and even location.Trader Joe's has built a brand as a supermarket selling niche food products you can't find at other supermarkets, their stores are moderately sized and have been deliberately located in more affluent urban neighborhoods, or seemingly so. In contrast Walmart is that one stop superstore that provides a large selection of low cost goods and is both a supermarket and general merchandise retailer. It has built a brand on affordablilty where a family or individuals dollar goes further and its sotres are found EVERYWHERE but particularily seem to be found in more suburban and low income areas.

  • Our goal and the aim of this project was to determine if a connection exists between home value and the number of Trader Joe's and/or Walmart's in proximity to the home.

  • What is the correlation between house location and proximity to Trader Joe's or Walmart?


The Data

  • Realtor.com data:
    • A real estate dataset with property listings for the entire U.S. and Puerto Rico with price(Target Value), number of bedrooms and baths, house size, lot size and the address.

  • Walmart data:
    • Kaggle dataset with location information(address, zip code, city, state, latitude and longitude) for all U.S. Walmart stores.

  • Trader Joe's data:
    • Kaggle dataset with location infromation(address, zip code, city, state, latitude and longitude) for all U.S. Trader Joe's.

Model Features



What's the correlation Matrix telling us?





  • Reviewing the correlation of our features we can see that Walmart and Trader Joe's proximities have correlation coefficients of -.14 and .23 respectively. These correlations are not incredibly strong but the only feature in our model with a greater correlation is the number of beds at .29.


  • Despite these low values the direction of the correlations matched what we predicted. We were expecting poperty values to be negatively correlated with the Walmart proiximty(how close a home is to Walmart) and positively correlated with proximity to Trader Joe's, and thats indeed what we see.


  • Another feature worth noting from the correlation matrix is Acre Lot which is showing no correlation. Inuitively this doesn't make sense, large multi-acre properties should be more valuable and we would expect to see a positive correlation. The little to no correlation we're seeing is likely due to the property types we examined in our model, more properties with little or no acres like apartments and condos. More research is needed though.
